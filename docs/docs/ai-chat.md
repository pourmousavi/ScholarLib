# AI Chat

Ask questions about your papers and get AI-powered answers with citations. The AI searches your documents, retrieves relevant passages, and generates answers based on your actual research.

## Chat Scope

Control which documents the AI searches when answering. The scope selector appears above the chat input:

| Scope | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **This doc** | Only the currently selected document |
| **Folder** | All documents in the current folder |
| **All** | Your entire library |
| **Tags** | Documents with specific tags you select |
| **Collections** | Documents in specific collections you select |

## Using Tags & Collections Scope

Tags and Collections scope options appear when you have tags/collections in your library:

1. Click **Tags** or **Collections** in the scope selector
2. A dropdown appears — check the tags/collections you want to include
3. If you select multiple, choose the mode:
   - **AND:** Documents must match ALL selected tags/collections
   - **OR:** Documents matching ANY selected tag/collection
4. The dropdown shows how many documents match your selection

:::tip Project-Focused Research
This is especially powerful for project-focused research. Scope to a collection like "Thesis Chapter 3" to ask questions only about papers relevant to that chapter.
:::

## How It Works

1. Your question is used to search relevant passages in the scoped documents
2. The most relevant chunks are retrieved (semantic search)
3. Retrieved context is sent to the AI along with your question
4. AI generates an answer based on your actual papers, with citations

## Quick Prompts

Use the suggested prompts for common research questions:

- "Summarise key findings"
- "What methods were used?"
- "List main conclusions"
- "Identify research gaps"
- "Compare approaches across papers"

## Chat History

- Conversations are automatically saved
- Click the **clock icon** in the sidebar to view history
- Resume any previous conversation with its original scope
- Export conversations as text for your notes

## Tips

:::tip Getting Better Answers
- Be specific in your questions for better answers
- Use "Folder" scope when comparing papers in the same research area
- Use "Collections" scope when working on a specific project
- Use "Tags" scope to explore a topic across your whole library
- Start a new conversation (+) when changing topics or scope
- Narrow your scope for faster, more focused answers
:::
